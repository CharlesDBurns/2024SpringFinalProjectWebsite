Gear

If only camping the gear needed is basic and minamal, being the base for other outdoor trips that may last for a few days, like fishing and canoeing. The gear needed for camping can vary from person to person depending on what kind of outdoor experience one wants to have. Gear can help make the experience easier with tools and comforts and likewise, the lack of gear can give a bit of challenge to the outing, which some seek. Of course as with any product, there should be some searching to find the certain product that meets one's wants or needs to make sure that they get what they specifically need. This is a list of basic items that one should consider before their camping trip. Below the information is a list of the items on this page that allows you to keep track of what you have already gotten.

Below is the basic gear one would generally pack for a planned camping trip.

  • Of course the first thing you'd think about when you think about camping is tents and for a good reason too. Tents are an essential for camping, they provide shelter out in nature where it might be hard to find for a new or even average camper. Tents are an absolute basic for anyone who wants to go camping. It's important to consider the size of tent for your needs as well as what conditions you want to be prepared for as some tents are more resilent to the elements than others. The more resilent tents however tend to be more expensive so strongly consider your needs before deciding on a tent.

  • Sleeping Bags are equally crucial to tents for anyone camping. After all, no one likes to sleep on the ground outside, it's just not comfortable. Sleeping Bags come in an array of sizes so not only do you need to search for a decent quality sleeping bag, you need to make sure you know what size you'd require. Generally when picking a sleeping bag size it should match as closely as it can to your height, but they tend to come in small (5 feet), regular (6 feet), and large (6 feet 6 inches).

  • Depending on how long you plan on camping or what you want your camping experience to be, decides whether you would bring food on your trip. Generally you'd want to bring canned or jarred foods that are not easily accessible by wildlife. However if you are not camping for very long other, more loosely packaged foods may be brought but should be kept in a safe place away from where wildlife may be able to get to it. Also be aware that some parks or reserves may limit or prohibit the food you may bring into the camping site.

  • Depending on the experience you want you may forego toiletries but most would want to maintain these comforts. Toiletries can vary for your prefrences and uses, but the main ones that people bring are things like toilet paper, toothbrush, and toothpaste. First Aid kits are also a big item to take on a camping trip to deal with unexpected accidents.

  • A lighter can be very useful in starting fires for warmth and cooking for various foods. Some may want a more survivalist experience and choose to go without and attempt to start a fire without, but generally campers bring a lighter.

  • Repellants are an important and sometimes essential item to bring on a camping trip as it can help make the experience much more enjoyable. There are various repellants for different kinds of wildlife but the most prominent repellant is bug repellant that keeps away pesky bugs from you while you camp. Bugs can be the most annoying creatures you encounter out while you're camping and having some repellant can make them irrelavent. There are also other repellants like bear repellant that may be of your interest if your camping destination is where bears may be encountered.
